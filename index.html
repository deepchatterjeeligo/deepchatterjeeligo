Hello! I am a postdoctoral fellow at the Center of AstroPhysical Surveys (CAPS) at NCSA, U. Illinois.

I work on gravitational-wave astronomy and astrophysics. My research is on the emerging field of multi-messenger astronomy with gravitational waves. Today, astronomy has reached an era of big data. The nature of research in this era has gotten me interested in bayesian analysis, machine-learning, and DevOps tools.

I love math, and the application of math in the physics of the big and small ranging from everyday life to celestial. It is the reason why I chose to study physics. While my work is in astrophysics, I am curious about other fields of physics. If there is an interesting class/lecture offered, I take it (time permit). I obtained my Ph.D. in physics at U. Wisconsin Milwaukee. Before that I did my integrated BS-MS in physics from Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER), Kolkata, India.

I am from Kolkata, native Bengali speaker, fluent in English and Hindi. I follow a small amount of Spanish (work in progress). Apart from work, I love listening to music, play guitar, and sing along. I also like to read, cook, and travel. Feel free to look around and contact me if you find bugs or wanted to chat.
